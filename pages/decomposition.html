Theory and Formula Guide

Even Signal

A signal is even when it is symmetric about the vertical axis, so values at +t and -t match.
x_e(t) = (x(t) + x(-t)) / 2
Add the signal to its mirrored version, then divide by 2 to extract the symmetric part.

Odd Signal

A signal is odd when it has origin symmetry, meaning the mirrored value changes sign.
x_o(t) = (x(t) - x(-t)) / 2
Subtract the mirrored signal and divide by 2 to isolate the antisymmetric component.

Reconstruction

Every signal can be written as the sum of its even and odd parts.
x(t) = x_e(t) + x_o(t)
When you add the two components back, the original waveform is recovered exactly.
